<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Many teams remain bogged down by operational chaos and manual drudgery, even with access to a variety of automation solutions. These tools often operate in silos, creating disconnected</span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/blog/ai/connect-your-islands-of-automation-with-ai-agents"> <span style="font-weight: 400;">islands of automation</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> that require significant human effort to bridge. </span></p>
<p><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/resources/ai/learn/what-is-agentic-ai/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Agentic AI</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> offers a path forward, creating a cohesive system that can intelligently and autonomously handle complex operational workflows. This guide provides a straightforward, five-step process for how to automate critical work with AI agents, enhancing both team productivity and operational resilience.</span></p>
<h2><b>Step 1. Identify high-value automation opportunities</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Before you can automate, you must identify which tasks have the highest return on your investment. Focus on workflows that are repetitive, time-consuming, and prone to human error. By analyzing daily operations and incident patterns, you can identify key areas. But be mindful, automating an imperfect process will escalate its flaws at a high pace rather than correcting them.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Focus on these common areas for automation:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Repetitive diagnostics:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Running standard commands and gathering data every time a specific alert fires, providing engineers with immediate context.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Manual triage:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Using AI to analyze, enrich, and</span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/blog/automation/automated-diagnostics-triage-the-fastest-way-to-cut-incident-time"> <span style="font-weight: 400;">route incoming alerts</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to the correct team based on severity and service ownership.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Stakeholder communication:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Automatically compiling and distributing status updates during and after incidents, keeping everyone informed without distracting engineers.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Post-incident activities:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Generating post-incident review summaries and documenting key events to speed up learning and follow-up actions.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Automating these tasks allows your top engineering talent to concentrate on innovation and improvements rather than constant problem-solving.</span></p>
<h2><b>Step 2. Map your critical workflows</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Once you identify a candidate for automation, you must map the existing workflow from start to finish. Understanding every step, decision, and handoff is key to effective automation. The incident response lifecycle is an excellent starting point. Significant risks arise from incomplete or inaccurate maps, which can cause automation to be brittle and collapse under pressures.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Document what happens when an alert is triggered. Assess by asking questions like:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Who receives the notification? </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">What data is required for diagnosis? </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Who needs to approve a proposed fix? </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This mapping process reveals the complexity and hidden dependencies that AI agents can manage. Pay special attention to the key decision points where an engineer must choose whether to</span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/blog/ai/ai-agents-incident-response-automate-vs-escalate"> <span style="font-weight: 400;">automate vs. escalate</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">These are perfect opportunities for an AI agent to step in, either by resolving the issue autonomously or by routing it to the right human expert with full context.</span></p>

<h2><b>Step 4. Configure, test, and build trust.</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The goal is intelligent augmentation, not risky, all-or-nothing replacement. You do not need to</span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/platform/automation"> <span style="font-weight: 400;">automate everything at once</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Begin by using a low-code platform to configure a simple workflow. A resource like the </span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/prompt-library/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">PagerDuty Prompt Library</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can accelerate this process by providing pre-built templates for common tasks.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Safety, security, and trust are paramount when deploying AI agents into production environments. The primary tradeoff you must manage is between agent autonomy and human control.</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Implement strict guardrails:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Agents must operate on a principle of least-privilege, granting them only the permissions necessary to perform their assigned tasks. Allowing too many permissions leads to considerable security vulnerabilities.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Maintain human oversight:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Set up workflows so that an agent proposes an action, such as &#8220;Restart a service,&#8221; which requires human approval before being carried out. This model balances speed with safety, keeping your experts in control.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Test and audit thoroughly:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Validate agent logic using historical data and real-world scenarios before full deployment. All agent actions should be logged and auditable, which will enhance trust and aid in troubleshooting.</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><b>Step 5. Deploy, monitor, and scale your automation</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Once your AI agents are deployed, it’s critical to measure their impact on key operational and business metrics. Tracking improvements in </span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/resources/devops/learn/what-is-mttr/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Mean Time To Resolve (MTTR),</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> reductions in human escalations, and gains in developer productivity provides clear evidence of your automation&#8217;s value. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Steer clear of vanity metrics and focus on prioritizing outcomes that show business impact.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This performance data builds the business case for scaling AI across more teams and workflows. Use the insights from your initial deployments to identify the next set of opportunities and drive broader adoption. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The long-term vision is an intelligent, automated operation where your teams are freed from manual toil to focus on strategic work, supported by a reliable digital workforce. By automating key workflows, you can </span><a href="https://www.pagerduty.com/resources/digital-operations/learn/incident-response-lifecycle-for-devops/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">transform the incident lifecycle</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and build a more resilient organization.</span></p>
